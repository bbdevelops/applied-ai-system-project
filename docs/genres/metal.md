# Metal

Metal is a high-intensity guitar-driven genre defined by distorted electric guitars (often down-tuned), aggressive drumming with double-bass kicks and blast beats, prominent bass, and vocal styles ranging from melodic clean singing to harsh screaming or growling. Tempos commonly run 110-200+ BPM, with energy values pushed to the maximum (0.85-0.99) and very low acousticness.

Subgenres include thrash, death, black, doom, power, progressive, and metalcore — each emphasizing different aspects of the form (speed, complexity, atmosphere, melody). Lyrically the genre explores aggression, mythology, philosophy, social criticism, and inner darkness. Metal rewards listeners who want music with maximum intensity, technical instrumental performance, and an uncompromising emotional palette.
