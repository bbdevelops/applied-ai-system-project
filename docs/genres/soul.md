# Soul

Soul music emerged in the 1960s from the fusion of gospel, R&B, and jazz, defined by passionate vocal performances, horn sections, gospel-rooted call-and-response structures, and tight rhythm sections. Tempos range 70-120 BPM, with energy varying from slow ballads to upbeat dance numbers. Acousticness is typically moderate to high — the genre is band-driven, not synth-driven.

Lyrical content centers on love, faith, social consciousness, and personal struggle, delivered with vocal intensity that distinguishes the genre from cooler, more restrained pop. Modern neo-soul preserves the form while adding hip-hop and electronic textures. Soul rewards listeners who want music with emotional weight, vocal virtuosity, and a strong rhythmic foundation.
