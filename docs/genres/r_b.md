# R&B

R&B (Rhythm and Blues) is a vocal-led genre rooted in soul, funk, and gospel traditions, defined by smooth melodic singing, syncopated grooves, and emotionally direct lyrics. Modern R&B blends acoustic instrumentation (piano, electric piano, bass) with programmed drums and synth textures. Tempos cluster 70-100 BPM with moderate energy and high danceability.

The genre's emotional range is broad but reliably intimate — romantic, sensual, melancholic, or quietly defiant — and the vocal performance carries most of the emotional weight, with melismatic runs and dynamic range central to the form. R&B rewards listeners drawn to vocal craft and grooves that prioritize feel over impact.
