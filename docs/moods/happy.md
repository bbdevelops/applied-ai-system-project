# Happy

A "happy" mood in music is signaled by bright timbres, major-key harmony, fast-but-comfortable tempos (95-130 BPM), strong rhythmic momentum, and lyrical content that's optimistic, celebratory, or gratitude-focused. Happy songs tend to score very high on valence (the musical-positivity axis), moderate-to-high on energy, and high on danceability. Production usually favors clarity over density, with prominent vocal hooks and uncomplicated structures.

Happy music is the default mood for celebration, social gatherings, exercise, road trips, and morning routines. It rewards listeners who want immediate emotional uplift without complexity — music designed to amplify a good feeling rather than complicate it.
