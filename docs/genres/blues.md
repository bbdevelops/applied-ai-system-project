# Blues

Blues is an African American musical tradition originating in the Deep South in the late 19th century, defined by the 12-bar blues chord progression, blue notes (flattened thirds, fifths, and sevenths), call-and-response phrasing, and lyrics rooted in personal hardship, longing, and resilience. Instrumentation is typically guitar-led (electric or acoustic), with harmonica, piano, bass, and drums. Tempos range 60-130 BPM.

The genre is the foundational ancestor of rock, R&B, soul, and hip-hop, and its vocal phrasing and improvisational structure echo through all of them. Blues rewards listeners drawn to emotional directness, vocal expressiveness, and a slow-burn rhythmic groove. The dominant moods are melancholic, soulful, defiant, or quietly hopeful.
