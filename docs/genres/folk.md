# Folk

Folk music is built on acoustic instrumentation — fingerpicked or strummed acoustic guitar, banjo, fiddle, mandolin, upright bass — with the singer-songwriter and storytelling tradition at its center. Tempos run slow to moderate (60-110 BPM), with very high acousticness, low instrumentalness (vocals dominate), and energy values varying from contemplative to driving.

Lyrical content prioritizes narrative, social commentary, place, and personal reflection over hooks or beats. Modern folk overlaps heavily with indie and Americana, retaining the acoustic palette but layering in light percussion or strings. The genre rewards listeners who care about lyrics and want unhurried, organic music — often nostalgic, melancholic, or quietly hopeful in mood.
