# Indie Pop

Indie pop sits in the middle ground between mainstream pop's hook-driven accessibility and indie rock's DIY, guitar-driven sensibility. Production is typically lighter and more textural than mainstream pop — jangly guitars, twee synths, restrained drums — with vocal melodies that prioritize understatement over big choruses. Tempos run 95-130 BPM with moderate energy and high vocal clarity.

Lyrically the genre tends toward introspection, romance, and small everyday observations rather than universal anthems. Indie pop sits comfortably in moods like "happy but a little wistful," "uplifting but reserved," or "nostalgic." It rewards listeners who want pop's craft without its scale — music that feels personal and slightly under-produced rather than maximalist.
