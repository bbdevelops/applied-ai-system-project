# Melancholic

Melancholic music conveys quiet sadness, longing, or reflective grief — minor harmonies, slow-to-mid tempos (60-100 BPM), low valence, low-to-moderate energy, and lyrical content focused on loss, distance, regret, or unspoken feeling. Production typically emphasizes space and restraint: sparse arrangements, long sustains, atmospheric reverb, vocal performances that lean into vulnerability rather than power.

Common across indie, folk, ambient, R&B ballads, and slowcore. Melancholic music is distinct from "sad" in that it carries a contemplative, almost beautiful quality — not pure pain, but acknowledgment. Listeners reach for it not to fix a feeling but to sit with it, often during winter, late nights, or transitional life periods.
