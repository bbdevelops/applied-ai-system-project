# Lofi

Lofi (short for "low fidelity") is a genre defined by its embrace of imperfection: tape hiss, vinyl crackle, slightly off-tempo drum loops, dusty piano samples, and warm, intentionally muffled mixing. Tempos sit slow, usually 60-90 BPM, with high acoustic and instrumental content and minimal or no vocals. Energy is intentionally low — the music is built to fade into the background rather than command attention.

Originally rooted in early hip-hop sampling tradition and Japanese ambient instrumental work, lofi has become inseparable from study, focus, and late-night work culture, popularized by 24/7 streaming radio formats. It rewards listeners who need calm, repetitive, non-demanding sonic texture — peaceful by design, contemplative without being melancholic.
