# Ambient

Ambient music prioritizes texture, atmosphere, and slow-evolving sound over melody, beat, or song structure. Tempos are deliberately slow or non-rhythmic, with high instrumentalness and acousticness varying by sub-style (electronic ambient leans synth-heavy; acoustic ambient uses piano, strings, field recordings). Energy is consistently low. The genre traces its roots to Brian Eno's 1970s "as ignorable as it is interesting" work and has since branched into dark ambient, ambient electronic, drone, and new-age variants.

Ambient rewards listeners who want music as environment rather than focus object — for sleep, meditation, deep work, or simply to color a room. It doesn't demand attention but rewards it, with details emerging over long listening sessions.
