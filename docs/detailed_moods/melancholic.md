# Melancholic (detailed mood)

The "melancholic" detailed-mood tag identifies songs whose center is reflective sadness — not pure grief and not despair, but a contemplative awareness of loss, distance, or unspoken longing. Sonic markers: minor harmony (often modal), slow-to-mid tempo (60-100 BPM), low valence, sparse arrangements, vocal performances that lean into vulnerability rather than power, and significant use of negative space.

Distinct from generally "sad" music because melancholic carries a quiet beauty — listeners often describe such songs as cathartic rather than draining. Heavy in indie folk, slowcore, certain R&B ballads, ambient. People reach for melancholic music not to fix a feeling but to acknowledge and sit with it, often during transitions or solitary time.
