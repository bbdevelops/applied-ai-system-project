# Polka

Polka is a lively dance music genre originating in 19th-century Bohemia (modern Czech Republic), characterized by fast 2/4 tempo (typically 120-180 BPM), bouncy upbeat rhythm, and prominent accordion, brass, clarinet, and tuba instrumentation. Energy is consistently high; danceability is the entire point. Acousticness is high, with traditional folk-band textures dominating.

The genre spread widely through Central European immigration to the Americas, evolving into regional subforms (Texas-Czech, Slovenian-style, Mexican norteño-polka). Lyrical content when present is celebratory, festive, often tied to weddings, harvests, or community gatherings. Polka rewards listeners drawn to energetic, joyful, communal dance music with deep folk roots — uplifting and unpretentious.
