# Aggressive (detailed mood)

A song carries the "aggressive" detailed-mood tag when its core emotional gesture is attack — distorted, dissonant, fast, and loud — without the softening or atmospheric framing that other intense music styles provide. Sonic markers: heavy distortion, percussive vocal delivery (shouting, screaming, harsh raps), pounding drums (often double-bass kicks), and structural choices that prioritize impact over dynamics.

Tempos commonly 140-220 BPM; energy near maximum; valence variable but often low. Heaviest in metal, hardcore punk, drill rap, and some industrial electronic. Aggressive songs are typically chosen as emotional channeling tools — a controlled exposure to one's own anger or stress that allows it to release through the song rather than at people.
