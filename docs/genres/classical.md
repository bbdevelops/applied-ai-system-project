# Classical

Classical music spans roughly 1000 years of European art music, from medieval chant through Baroque, Classical, Romantic, and modern eras. The genre is defined by acoustic instrumentation (orchestra, chamber ensembles, solo piano), notated composition, and structural complexity (sonata form, symphonies, concertos, fugues). Tempos vary enormously by piece and movement; energy ranges from contemplative to intensely dramatic.

Vocals are absent in most instrumental works; when present (opera, art song, choral) they are highly trained and operatic in style. Acousticness is at the maximum, instrumentalness is high, danceability typically low. Classical rewards focused, attentive listening; it scales from background concentration music (Bach, Satie) to commanding emotional pieces (Beethoven, Mahler) requiring full attention.
