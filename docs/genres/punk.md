# Punk

Punk rock emerged in the mid-1970s as a stripped-down, fast, aggressive reaction against the technical excess of mainstream rock. Defining traits: loud distorted guitars (often three-chord), driving drums, raw production, short song lengths (typically under three minutes), and shouted or yelled vocals. Tempos commonly 130-200 BPM; energy maxed, acousticness near zero, danceability moderate (mosh-pit oriented rather than dance-floor).

Lyrically punk is direct, angry, often political — anti-authority, anti-corporate, pro-DIY. Subgenres include hardcore, pop-punk, post-punk, and ska-punk, but the unifying ethos is energy, immediacy, and refusal of polish. Punk rewards listeners who want short, intense, urgent music that feels more like a statement than a craft.
