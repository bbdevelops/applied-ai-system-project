# Moody

"Moody" music creates a charged, atmospheric emotional climate without committing to a single feeling — darker timbres, minor harmony, restrained dynamics, and a sense of held tension. Tempos typically slow-to-mid (70-110 BPM), with low-to-moderate energy and ambiguous valence. Vocals are often expressive but understated; production leans on space, reverb, and texture rather than impact.

Heavily represented in alternative R&B, trip-hop, dark indie, post-rock, and certain electronic subgenres. Moody is broader than melancholic — it can be sensual, introspective, ominous, or contemplative depending on context. It rewards listeners who want music that holds emotional space without forcing resolution: night drives, alone time, slow evenings.
