# Jazz

Jazz is an improvisation-centered genre rooted in early 20th-century African American musical tradition, defined by swing rhythm, syncopation, and the conversational interplay between soloists. Acoustic instruments dominate — piano, upright bass, drums, saxophone, trumpet, guitar — with tempos ranging from slow ballads (60-80 BPM) to fast bebop (200+ BPM). Energy varies enormously by subgenre: cool jazz and ballad jazz are calm and contemplative; hard bop and free jazz are intense.

Modern jazz often blurs into lofi, hip-hop, and ambient, but retains the focus on instrumental virtuosity and improvisation. It rewards focused, attentive listening — the genre rarely seeks to overwhelm; instead it invites the listener into a shared musical conversation.
