# Angry

Angry music expresses frustration, resentment, or grievance — directly through lyrical content and indirectly through musical choices: minor key, dissonant chords, aggressive dynamics, sharp vocal delivery (shouted, screamed, or sneered). Energy is high, valence is low, and tempos typically range 110-180 BPM. Production often emphasizes texture roughness over polish — distortion, raw mixing, less restraint.

Distinct from "aggressive" in that angry leans inward (the speaker's grievance) rather than outward (attack). Common to punk, certain rock subgenres, drill rap, and emotionally raw indie. Listeners reach for angry music to acknowledge and process anger rather than suppress it — to feel heard by the song, not necessarily energized by it.
